    Oil prices little changed as markets look to OPEC+ meeting

    Oil prices were in a holding pattern in early Asian trading on Monday as markets awaited an OPEC+ meeting on June 2 where producers are expected to discuss maintaining voluntary output cuts for the rest of the year. U.S. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures rose 13 cents to $77.85. The upcoming meeting of the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries and allies, known as OPEC+, was pushed back by a day to June 2 and will be held online, OPEC said on Friday.
